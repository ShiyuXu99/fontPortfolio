Introduction

Bar

The bar is the horizontal strike in a character. For example, H, F, and A all contain a bar.

Tail

The decorative curved descender of a capital Q, R and K. The descenders of the lower case g, j, p, q, and y are also sometimes called tails.

Stem

The stem is the core part of most letters with a vertical or diagonal stroke.

Counter

Counters are the negative spaces inside letter form, in other words, a counter is the area of a letter that is entirely or partially enclosed by a letter

Ear

Ear is the small stroke that projects from the top of the lowercase g.

Ball Terminal

The decorative curved descender of a capital Q, R and K. The descenders of the lower case g, j, p, q, and y are also sometimes called tails.

Bowl

A design feature of a typeface or glyph where the end of a stroke takes a roughly circular shape.

Descender

The imaginary line depicting the distance between the baseline and the bottom of the descender.

Shoulder

A curved stroke originating from a stem, the round part of character.

Ascender

Ascenders are the vertical, upwards strokes that rise above the x-height.

X-height

X-height is the distance between the baseline and the mean line of lower-case letters in a typeface
